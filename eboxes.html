BRAINBOX

Where it all happens

Berlin already has the right infrastructure to host the E-lympics. Developed by researchers at the Berlin Institute of Technology, the BrainBox is an interactive environment that allows the creation of virtual reality scenarios. It consists of a cubic structure with a default size of 49m2, which has the possibility to be expanded.

Treadmills for virtual gaming can be placed in the internal corners of the Box, allowing participants to engage in virtual versions of the Olympic Games such as running, boxing and fencing. Viewers would be able to observe the players and follow the virtual match through giant screens placed in the walls of the Box. A powerful sound system contributes for a true group experience.

Furthermore, viewers equipped with gadgets, such as virtual glasses and high tech shirts, could put themselves in the shoes of Olympic athletes while watching the official competition live, broadcast at the Box. Designed to be “plug and play”, the BrainBox can be set up anywhere in Berlin. The only requirements are fast internet and electricity.
